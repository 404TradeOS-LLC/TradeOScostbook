# TradeOS Bible — Volume 6: Founder

## Purpose

This volume preserves founder intent, decision boundaries, unresolved choices, and launch commitments so product direction does not disappear between sessions or get replaced by agent assumptions.

## Founder intent

TradeOS is a real business, not an experiment. Engineering work should create a product that can be demonstrated, beta-tested, sold, supported, and improved without losing operational clarity.

## Founder priorities

- useful owner/admin experience instead of blank or disconnected screens;
- strong estimating and cost intelligence;
- practical scheduling and dispatcher workflows;
- customer-facing documents that look professional and carry the contractor's brand;
- AI that helps prepare work without silently committing it;
- a product that is easier to adopt than enterprise competitors;
- durable documentation so work continues without repeated re-explanation.

## Standing decisions

### AI behavior

AI assistance is review-first. It may draft, resolve, summarize, prioritize, and recommend. Accepted consequential changes must pass normal service, authorization, validation, and audit boundaries.

### Product complexity

Do not solve every possible future contractor need in the first release. Prefer a complete, understandable workflow over broad unfinished surface area.

### Founder Preview

The founder must be able to enter the product and see a useful, nonblank operational experience with realistic current-state framing. Preview behavior must not pretend unavailable integrations or domain models exist.

### Branding

TradeOS must support contractor identity across product and customer-facing surfaces. Known gaps between Settings branding, Brand Studio, portal rendering, and generated documents must be tracked rather than hidden.

### Execution

Agents should follow the Bible and sprint queue. The founder should not have to reconstruct scope, branch strategy, tests, and handoff language for each session.

## Founder decisions required

A sprint must explicitly stop for founder input when it changes:

- target customer or market position;
- pricing or packaging;
- public product promises;
- destructive migration policy;
- core lifecycle meaning;
- branding architecture consolidation;
- autonomous AI authority;
- launch timing or release criteria;
- major third-party platform commitment;
- scope beyond the approved roadmap.

## Decision record format

When a founder decision is required, record:

- decision ID and date;
- question;
- available options;
- recommendation;
- founder decision;
- rationale;
- affected sprints and documents;
- revisit trigger.

## Idea intake

New ideas belong in a controlled intake section before they enter the sprint backlog. Each idea should state customer value, evidence, dependencies, expected scope, and what current work it would displace.

## Launch commitment

TradeOS should launch only when the founder can confidently demonstrate the core workflow, explain the value, onboard a contractor, support early users, and trust the security and financial behavior of the platform.
