# TradeOS Bible — Volume 5: Business

## Purpose

This volume defines how TradeOS becomes a durable business: target market, positioning, packaging, pricing logic, sales motion, beta strategy, and launch discipline.

## Market position

TradeOS targets contractors who have outgrown basic field-service software but are not ready for enterprise cost, implementation time, or complexity.

## Ideal customer profile

- 5–30 technicians as the initial core segment;
- owner-led or operations-led trade business;
- meaningful estimating, scheduling, invoicing, and customer-document volume;
- current tool fragmentation or dissatisfaction;
- willingness to participate in migration and workflow onboarding;
- need for stronger operational visibility without enterprise overhead.

## Competitive frame

TradeOS must beat lightweight tools on depth and enterprise tools on usability, implementation burden, transparency, and price-to-value.

## Commercial pillars

### Clear value

The product must tie features to revenue, margin, speed, control, or customer trust.

### Transparent packaging

Pricing and feature boundaries should be understandable without a sales-engineering process.

### Migration confidence

Contractors need a credible path from spreadsheets, Jobber, Housecall Pro, Buildertrend, ServiceTitan, or other systems.

### Fast first value

Onboarding should produce useful customers, projects, estimates, jobs, or dashboard insight quickly.

### Proof before scale

Beta evidence, workflow completion, reliability, and customer feedback should precede aggressive acquisition spending.

## Beta strategy

Initial beta should prioritize known contractors who can provide direct workflow feedback. Beta success is measured by completed real workflows, not account creation alone.

Required beta evidence includes:

- successful onboarding;
- customer and project setup;
- estimate and proposal completion;
- job scheduling and assignment;
- invoice readiness and payment-state handling;
- owner dashboard usefulness;
- support burden and defect severity;
- willingness to continue using the product.

## Sales narrative

TradeOS is the contractor operating system that helps growing trade businesses estimate accurately, coordinate work, act on what needs attention, and move money through the business without enterprise complexity.

## Business metrics

Track at minimum:

- activated organizations;
- time to first useful workflow;
- weekly active owners/dispatchers;
- estimates created and accepted;
- jobs scheduled and completed;
- invoices issued and paid;
- AI suggestions reviewed and accepted;
- support incidents;
- retention and expansion;
- acquisition cost and payback once paid growth begins.

## Launch gates

Do not call the product launch-ready until security, tenancy, lifecycle consistency, deployment, migration posture, support workflow, pricing, onboarding, and beta evidence are documented and accepted.
